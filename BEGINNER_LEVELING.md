# Leveling guide for the total beginner

This is the long, patient version. It assumes you have never played
Path of Exile. You don't need to memorize any of it — the helper card
on your screen always tells you the next step. This guide explains
**what the words mean and why you're doing it**, so the card's short
instructions make sense. Read the Basics once; use the act-by-act
part as a companion while you play.

(Set up the helper card first — that's `START_HERE_EASY.md`.)

## The shape of the game

The campaign is **10 acts**. Every act works the same way:

1. You arrive in a **town**. Towns are safe — nothing can hurt you
   there. Towns have people (NPCs) who give quests, sell things, and
   hand out rewards.
2. You fight through a chain of **zones** (the wilderness/dungeon
   areas between towns), doing a few quests on the way.
3. At the end of the act there's a **boss**. You kill it, walk
   through the door behind it, and you're in the next act.

Repeat ten times, and you win the campaign. With a party and the
card, that's roughly a weekend of relaxed play.

## The golden rules

If you remember nothing else, remember these:

1. **Do what the card says, in order.** It never asks for anything
   optional-but-useless; if a detour is listed, it pays (usually a
   permanent character upgrade).
2. **Stay near your party.** Most of what your build does helps the
   people around you just by you standing there.
3. **Keep moving in fights.** Almost everything that would kill you
   is dropped on the ground where you were standing a second ago.
4. **Press your flasks.** (More below — this is the #1 thing new
   players forget.)
5. **When your level is close to the zone's level, you're fine.**
   The card warns you when it isn't (the ⚠ XP line).
6. **You can't really lose.** Dying just puts you back in town —
   in the first half of the game it costs nothing but the walk back.
   Your items and progress are never lost.
7. **Lost? Press F7.** The little maps panel shows the zone's shape
   and the green line is the way out. Or just ask — "where do I go"
   is a normal thing to say in party chat.
8. **Don't hoover up every item.** Point at a dropped item and press
   **Ctrl+C** — the card answers TAKE or SKIP.
9. **When you get to town, do the town list.** The card shows it:
   turn in quests, grab rewards, top up flasks, check the vendor.
10. **Have fun — nobody is grading you.** The party comp was chosen
    so that you being present is already a contribution.

## Basics, explained properly

### Your skills come from gems

In this game, abilities are **skill gems** — colored stones you
socket into your gear. Click an item's socket, put the gem in, and
the skill appears on your action bar.

- Gems and sockets have colors (red/green/blue). A gem only fits a
  matching-color socket.
- Some sockets are **linked** (a little bridge drawn between them).
  A **support gem** placed in a linked socket makes the skill next
  to it stronger. That's the whole "links" system people talk about:
  more linked sockets = more supports = stronger skill.
- Gems **level up on their own** while socketed. Never remove a gem
  to throw it away — it's been quietly growing this whole time.
- Where do gems come from? **Quest rewards** (the card says "take X
  from so-and-so") and **town vendors** — every act's town has
  someone who sells gems (Nessa in Act 1, **Yeena** in Act 2,
  Clarissa in Act 3, and so on). When the card says "buy X @16",
  that means: from level 16 on, that gem is available at the town
  gem-seller — grab it next time you're in town.

**Your personal gem plan** is the file `builds\<YourName>_plan.md`
(printable), and the card shows the same reminders act by act. You
never have to decide which gems to use — just take what it names.

### Flasks — press the buttons

The five slots at the bottom-left are **flasks**. They are not
emergency-only potions; they are meant to be used constantly.

- **Life flasks** heal you. Press one the moment your life bar dips
  past about two-thirds. Don't save them "for later" — they refill
  automatically as you kill things, and refill completely in town.
- The **Quicksilver flask** makes you run much faster. Press it
  basically whenever it's ready and you're moving between fights.
  The card makes sure you get one early in Act 1.
- In each new town, check the vendor for **bigger life flasks** —
  a few coins for a permanently better heal button. The card's town
  steps remind you.

### Your passive tree — one point per level

Every level-up gives one **passive skill point** (and quests give
**24 more** across the campaign — the act-by-act part below lists
every one, and the card flags each as it comes). Spend them
following your build's plan (`builds\<YourName>_plan.md` shows the
order; ask your party if unsure — allocating takes ten seconds). If
you misclick, don't panic: you also get free **respec points** from
quests, and nothing is ever ruined.

### Gear: only three things matter while leveling

Ignore 95% of what monsters drop. While leveling you want, in this
order:

1. **Life** ("+# to maximum Life") on every piece you can.
2. **Elemental resistances** ("+#% to Fire/Cold/Lightning
   Resistance"). Press **C** to open your character sheet and look:
   each resistance caps at **75%**. Near 75/75/75 = you're tanky.
   Deep negative = you'll get one-shot by that element.
3. **Movement speed on boots.** Any boots with "#% increased
   Movement Speed" are a quality-of-life upgrade.

If your damage feels weak and you attack with a weapon, a better
weapon fixes it; if you cast spells, your gem levels matter more
than your weapon. Your build plan says which camp you're in.
And remember: for any specific drop, **Ctrl+C** gives a verdict.

### Waypoints, portals, and the famous "logout"

- **Waypoints** are the blue circular platforms. Walking near one
  activates ("tags") it forever — you can then teleport between any
  tagged waypoints from any town. When the card says **"tag the
  waypoint"**, just walk over it. That's all.
- **Portal scrolls** open a personal blue portal to town and back.
  Also how you catch up to the party: party members' portals, and
  the "travel to party member" option (click their portrait), are
  free rides to wherever everyone else is.
- The card sometimes says **"LOGOUT to town"**. Yes, really: exit
  to the login screen and log back in. You reappear in town
  instantly with everything you looted. It's a completely allowed
  speed trick every experienced player uses — the card only asks
  for it when it saves a long walk back.

### Trials and the Labyrinth — do not skip these

Scattered through Acts 1–3 are six **Trials of Ascendancy** (the
card marks each one — they're short obstacle rooms, spikes and
traps). Completing all six unlocks **the Labyrinth**: a longer
gauntlet the party will run together around Act 4. Finishing it
grants your **Ascendancy** — your subclass, the biggest single
power-up in the campaign. Acts 6–10 contain six more trials for the
two harder Labyrinth difficulties later; the card schedules all of
it — just don't skip a step marked "Trial".

Traps in trials hurt a LOT more than monsters do. Walk them
patiently, one pattern at a time. There is no timer.

### Dying, and the XP warning

- Acts 1–5: dying costs nothing but the walk back. Genuinely.
- Acts 6–10: each death costs a small slice of experience (never a
  level, never items).
- The card's ⚠ XP line means the zone is far from your level (too
  low = little experience; too high = dangerous). If your party is
  rushing ahead and you see it a lot, say so — out-leveling the
  group helps nobody.

### Town business, once per town

When you arrive in a new town, the card lists it, but the routine
is always: **turn in quests → take the reward it names → buy any
gems it names → top up flasks → sell junk** (vendors buy anything;
sold something by mistake? the vendor's **buyback tab** undoes it).
Two minutes, then move on.

### Playing in a party

- Monsters get tougher for each party member, but your group is
  built for that — the roles cover each other. Your job is mostly
  to **exist near the group** (really — auras, banners and buffs
  radiate from you).
- The card's party row shows everyone's level and location; ● means
  in your zone, ☠ counts deaths (they flash red — check on them!).
- Fell behind? Waypoint to the zone they're in, or click a
  portrait → travel to party member. Nobody minds.
- One warning for later (Act 10+): if the screen says a rare
  monster has a **Proximity Shield**, you must stand INSIDE the
  bubble to damage it. Mostly your carry's problem, not yours.

## Act by act

This is the same route the overlay card follows, cross-checked
against poe-vault's [Quick Reference Leveling Guide](https://www.poe-vault.com/guides/quick-reference-leveling-guide-for-path-of-exile)
— if you like reading ahead with pictures, that's the companion
site. The "leave around level N" numbers come from this route's own
zone data; being a level or three **above** is completely fine
(normal, even, in a casual party), while more than ~4 **below**
means slow down and clear a bit more.

A note on reading the walkthroughs: they're the road map, not
homework. In a party you'll often just follow whoever's in front —
the value here is knowing *why* the group keeps ducking into
side rooms (it's almost always a free skill point or a trial).

### Act 1 — the beach (levels 1–12)

You wash ashore with nothing and fight down a beach; the game
starts gently, so use this act to practice flask-pressing and
Ctrl+C.

**The road:**

- **Twilight Strand** → kill Hillock at the end of the beach, enter
  town (**Lioneye's Watch**). Take your first skill gem from
  Tarkleigh.
- **The Coast** → tag the waypoint, then detour to **The Tidal
  Island**: kill Hailrake, loot the Medicine Chest, and logout.
  Nessa's reward is your **Quicksilver Flask** — put it on your bar
  and press it forever after.
- **The Mud Flats** → pull the three glyphs from the rhoa nests
  (quest-marked), which opens **The Submerged Passage**. If you
  spot the Flooded Depths entrance inside, dip in and kill the
  Dweller of the Deep — it pays a skill point in town.
- **The Ledge → The Climb → The Lower Prison** — your **first
  Trial of Ascendancy** is in the Lower Prison (a spike corridor;
  walk it patiently).
- **The Upper Prison** → boss: Brutus, a chained giant — keep
  moving so his hook-slam misses.
- **Prisoner's Gate → The Ship Graveyard** → find the Ship
  Graveyard Cave (loot the Allflame) and kill Captain Fairgraves.
  Back in town, a small pile of rewards is waiting — including
  movement-skill gems for most builds.
- **Cavern of Wrath → Cavern of Anger** → the act boss.

**Free skill points:** The Marooned Mariner (Bestel) and Deal with
the Dweller (Tarkleigh).

**Boss — Merveil:** two phases, heavy **cold** damage. Sidestep the
ice spears, don't stand in the geysers, and any cold resistance
you've picked up softens the whole fight. The passage behind her
arena leads to Act 2. *Leave around level 12.*

### Act 2 — the forest (levels 12–22)

A forest crossroads where three roads fan out from one hub zone.
This act has the campaign's one story choice — the bandits — and
your route already made it: **kill all three** (it's worth a
permanent skill point). Town gem-seller: **Yeena**.

**The road:**

- **The Old Fields** → on the way through, look for **The Den**:
  killing the Great White Beast inside earns a **second Quicksilver
  Flask** from Yeena.
- **The Crossroads** (hub — you'll be back) → north to the
  **Chamber of Sins**: **Trial #2** on level 2 (sawblades), then
  kill Fidelitas and loot the Baleful Gem.
- Back at the Crossroads: **The Broken Bridge** → bandit lord
  **Kraityn** — kill him.
- **Fellshrine Ruins → The Crypt**: **Trial #3** on level 1
  (spinning blades); on level 2, the Golden Hand sits on an altar
  behind an optional boss. Turning it in is a skill point.
- **The Riverways → The Western Forest** → two jobs here: bandit
  lord **Alira** in her camp (kill), and the Blackguard camp
  (kill the leader, use the Thaumetic Emblem on the sealed door).
  The **Weaver's Chambers** hides off this forest too — kill the
  Weaver for Maligaro's Spike (gem reward in town).
- **The Wetlands** → bandit lord **Oak** (kill). Hand all three
  bandit amulets to Eramir: **+1 skill point** and the Apex.
- **Vaal Ruins → Northern Forest → The Caverns → The Ancient
  Pyramid** → climb the four floors to the top.

**Free skill points:** all three bandits killed (Eramir), Through
Sacred Ground (Yeena), and The Way Forward — that one's collected
from Bestel back in the **Act 1** town; the card sends you through
the waypoint for it.

**Boss — the Vaal Oversoul:** a giant stone construct. Stay out
from directly underneath it, sidestep the slam and the falling
rocks, and don't stand in the lightning ground. *Leave around
level 22.*

### Act 3 — the city (levels 22–32)

A ruined imperial city — the longest-feeling act, and the one with
the most town-trips; the waypoints keep it tight. Three trials here
finish your set of six. Town gem-seller: Clarissa.

**The road:**

- **The Slums → The Crematorium**: **Trial #4** (furnace traps),
  then kill Piety (she flees) and take Tolman's Bracelet. Clarissa
  trades it for the **Sewer Keys**.
- **The Sewers** → grab the **three Platinum Busts** from the
  quest-marked stashes (that's Victario's Secrets — a skill point),
  tag the waypoint (you'll be back), exit to the **Marketplace**.
- **The Catacombs** (off the Marketplace): **Trial #5** (blade
  sentries).
- **The Battlefront** → loot the Ribbon Spool from the Blackguard
  chest (looks like a cart); **The Docks** → grab the Thaumetic
  Sulphite, then logout.
- **Solaris Temple** (two floors) → Lady Dialla swaps your bits for
  the **Infernal Talc**.
- **Sewers waypoint** → burn the Undying Blockage → **The Ebony
  Barracks**: kill General Gravicius. Then the **Lunaris Temple**
  (two floors — when in doubt, look for ascending staircases):
  Piety again, for real this time; she drops the Tower Key.
- **The Imperial Gardens** → **Trial #6** (dart traps). **That's
  all six — the Labyrinth is now unlocked** (its door is the
  Aspirants' Plaza waypoint; the party runs it early in Act 4).
- **The Sceptre of God** → climb both zones to the rooftop.

**Free skill points:** Victario's Secrets (Hargan) and Piety's Pets
(Grigor).

**Boss — Dominus:** the classic new-player wall. Phase 1: when he
raises his hand and the screen darkens — **run**. That's the
"Touch of God" slam, and it kills you if you stand there. Phase 2,
demon form: don't stand in the red blood rain; it stacks. The
passage beyond him leads to Act 4. *Leave around level 32.*

### Act 4 — the mountain (levels 32–40)

A short act inside a hollow mountain — but the important event is
right at the start: **the Labyrinth** (~level 33–35 is the sweet
spot). The party runs it together; at the end you pick your
**Ascendancy** — your subclass and the single biggest power jump
of the campaign. Then the act itself:

**The road:**

- **The Dried Lake** → kill Voll and loot Deshret's Banner; it
  breaks the seal on the Mines.
- **The Mines** (two levels) → on level 2, find Deshret's spirit
  and **free it** (skill point). Through to the **Crystal Veins**
  waypoint; Dialla wants two "eyes" from the dream worlds.
- **Kaom's Dream → Kaom's Stronghold** → kill King Kaom in his
  caldera (Eye of Fury). Tasuni's Book of Skill is waiting in town.
- **Daresso's Dream → The Grand Arena** → kill Daresso, the swords
  duelist (Eye of Desire). Hand Dialla both eyes; she fires the
  Rapture Device and opens... a wound in the mountain.
- **The Belly of the Beast** (two flesh-corridor levels) → kill
  Piety the Abomination, and **talk to her human form after** —
  it opens the way.
- **The Harvest** → kill the three organ guardians (Doedre,
  Shavronne, Maligaro), deliver the organs, and enter the Black
  Core.

**Free skill points:** An Indomitable Spirit (Deshret's spirit) and
Tasuni's Book of Skill after Kaom falls.

**Boss — Malachai:** two arenas. Phase 1: let Piety's beam strip
his immunity shield, dodge the teleport-slam. Phase 2 (the Black
Heart): when he drains one of the three Hearts of the Beast, kill
that heart — three hearts, then finish him. Stay off the pulsing
floor-growths. Afterwards you climb The Ascent and portal to
Oriath. *Leave around level 40.*

### Act 5 — the holy city (levels 40–45)

Oriath: the slaver city you were exiled from. Two big boss fights
book-end it, both **fire**-heavy — the card hands you a Ruby Flask
(fire resistance button) partway through; keep it.

**The road:**

- **The Slave Pens** → kill Overseer Krow, enter town (Overseer's
  Tower). Lani's ring reward: pick the one patching your worst
  resistance.
- **The Control Blocks** → grab the Miasmeter from the Experimental
  Supplies chest (left edge of the map, usually), kill Justicar
  Casticus for his Eyes of Zeal.
- **Oriath Square** → waypoint, logout, turn-ins (a skill point and
  that Ruby Flask), then the Eyes open the **Templar Courts** door.
- **The Chamber of Innocence** → kill High Templar Avarius; the god
  **Innocence** then manifests — heavy fire, stay out of the beams,
  keep circling.
- **The Torched Courts** (same courts, now burning) → **The Ruined
  Square** → kill Utula by the fountain, then duck into **The
  Reliquary** for the three Kitava's Torments (another skill point).
- **The Ossuary** → grab the Sign of Purity at the far end (the
  hidden passage by the tomb drops you straight back), then cross
  to **The Cathedral Rooftop**.

**Free skill points:** In Service to Science (Vilenta) and Kitava's
Torments (Lani).

**Boss — Kitava, the Insatiable:** a cathedral-sized demon and the
end of "part one". Near-constant fire; sidestep the scythe slams,
kill the little adds to keep your flasks charged, and don't panic
at the screen chaos — your party is doing the heavy lifting.
**After this fight, all your resistances permanently drop by 30%.**
That's the game's design, not something you broke — fixing resists
is the whole party's first job in Act 6, and your saved-up gear
stash suddenly matters (Ctrl+C everything). *Leave around
level 45.*

### Act 6 — the return (levels 45–50)

Back across the sea to Act 1's lands, now wrecked — you'll
recognize every zone, remixed. From here on deaths cost a little
XP, and resistances actually matter: press C now and then. This
act also introduces the **Pantheon** (minor god-powers you collect
from certain bosses — the card flags them; they're free passive
bonuses you toggle from a menu).

**The road:**

- **Twilight Strand** (again!) → clear it for Lilly Roth, collect a
  respec book in town.
- **The Coast → Mud Flats** → kill the Dishonored Queen (Eye of
  Conquest) → **The Karui Fortress**: fight up to Tukohama's Keep
  and kill **Tukohama** (your first Pantheon soul).
- **The Ridge → The Lower Prison** → **trial** (first of three for
  the Cruel Labyrinth) → **Shavronne's Tower**: Brutus round two on
  the rooftop, then **Shavronne**.
- **Prisoner's Gate** → the goat-god **Abberath** waits in the
  Valley of the Fire Drinker.
- **Western Forest → The Riverways** → the card routes you through
  this act's three named-monster hunts (the Father of War, the
  Puppet Mistress, the Cloven One) — each one pays a skill point
  back in town.
- **Southern Forest → Cavern of Anger** (grab the Black Flag) →
  **The Beacon**: escort the two pillars to their berths (stand in
  the glowing circles), light the beacon, and sail.

**Free skill points:** The Father of War (Tarkleigh), The Puppet
Mistress (Tarkleigh), The Cloven One (Bestel).

**Boss — Tsoagoth, the Brine King:** dodge the geysers and wave
slams, clear the drowned adds between phases, and don't stand at
the arena edge when the wave winds up. Weylam Roth then sails you
to Act 7. *Leave around level 50.*

### Act 7 — the spider forest (levels 50–55)

Act 2's forest, webbed over. Fair warning: this act ends in a
spider cave with a spider god — if that's genuinely unpleasant for
you, say so and follow the party's cleared path; nobody minds. Two
trials here (plus Act 6's) unlock the **Cruel Labyrinth** —
ascendancy points 3 and 4, run when the party's ready.

**The road:**

- **Broken Bridge → The Crossroads** → down to **Fellshrine
  Ruins → The Crypt**: **trial**, then the Maligaro's Map pickup.
- **Chamber of Sins** → the map goes into the Map Device on level
  1; it opens **Maligaro's Sanctum** — kill Maligaro. Then **trial**
  on level 2 (that completes the Cruel set).
- **The Den → Ashen Fields** → kill Greust → **Northern Forest →
  The Dread Thicket**: kill Gruthkul in the Den of Despair, and
  collect the **7 Fireflies** scattered around the thicket (quest-
  marked; they light Yeena's way later).
- **The Causeway** → grab **Kishara's Star** from its lockbox
  (usually right by the exit) → **The Vaal City** — find Yeena.
- **Temple of Decay** (two levels) → down into the web.

**Free skill points:** The Master of a Million Faces (Eramir),
Queen of Despair (Eramir), Kishara's Star (Weylam Roth).

**Boss — Arakaali, Spinner of Shadows:** big area blasts plus
waves of spiderlings. Never stand still; circle the arena edge and
let the party clear the adds. The exit beyond her web leads to
Act 8. *Leave around level 55.*

### Act 8 — the city at war (levels 55–59)

Sarn again, mid-civil-war between the sun goddess (Solaris) and
moon goddess (Lunaris) — you'll clear one temple for each. First
Merciless-Labyrinth trial here (the last set — they're for later).

**The road:**

- **Toxic Conduits → Doedre's Cesspool** → kill the witch Doedre
  over the cauldron.
- **The Quay** → the Ankh of Eternity is usually near the entrance
  bridge; it revives Tolman (kill what he becomes — that's Love is
  Dead, a skill point). → **The Grain Gate**: kill the Gemling
  Legionnaires as you cross (another point).
- **Imperial Fields → Solaris Temple** (two floors) → kill **Dawn**
  and take the **Sun Orb**. Turn-ins in town.
- **Solaris Concourse → Harbour Bridge → Lunaris Concourse** →
  **The Bath House**: **trial** (Merciless #1).
- **The High Gardens** → push to the Pools of Terror and kill
  **Yugul** (skill point via Sin).
- **Lunaris Temple** (two floors) → kill **Dusk** and take the
  **Moon Orb**.
- **Harbour Bridge** → place both orbs at the Sky Shrine altars to
  summon the goddesses.

**Free skill points:** Love is Dead (Clarissa), The Gemling Legion
(Maramoa), Reflection of Terror (Sin).

**Boss — Solaris & Lunaris:** alternating **fire** and **cold**
phases, then both at once. Long fight, but every attack is well
telegraphed — treat it as a dance, keep your flasks cycling.
*Leave around level 59.*

### Act 9 — the desert (levels 59–63)

Highgate's desert side: an oasis, a mine, and a lot of sand.
Everything purple in this act is **chaos damage** — it goes
straight past energy shield, so life flasks are your friend.

**The road:**

- **Blood Aqueduct** (a famously chill zone) → **The Descent →
  Vastiri Desert**: loot the Storm Blade from the Storm-Weathered
  Chest; Petarus and Vanja turn it into the Bottled Storm, which
  opens **The Oasis**.
- **The Oasis → Sand Pit** → kill **Shakari**, the scorpion god
  (Pantheon soul; skill point from Irasha).
- **The Foothills → The Boiling Lake** → kill the **Basilisk** and
  bottle its acid (it un-petrifies the Trarthan garrison).
- **The Tunnel** → **trial** (Merciless #2 — spawns left or right
  of the main path).
- **The Quarry** → two arenas branch off: **Garukhan**, Queen of
  the Winds (drops the Sekhema Feather — a skill point) and the
  **Refinery** (General Adus). Either order.
- **The Belly of the Beast → The Rotting Core** → meet Sin, then
  clear the three rotten wings — Doedre, Maligaro, and Shavronne
  again, each fused with a monster.

**Free skill points:** Queen of the Sands (Irasha) and the Sekhema
Feather (Irasha or Tasuni).

**Boss — the Depraved Trinity:** the three wing-bosses power the
final fight in the Black Heart. Chaos damage everywhere: don't
stand in anything purple, keep moving, and it folds. *Leave around
level 63.*

### Act 10 — the siege (levels 63–68)

Oriath, burning. One last trial, one last god, and the fight you
already won once — bigger this time.

**The road:**

- **Cathedral Rooftop** → kill Plaguewing and free Bannon.
- **The Ravaged Square** (hub — the north bridge is out, so the
  route winds) → **The Ossuary**: **trial** (Merciless #3 — the
  set is done), plus two quick quest pickups (a respec book and a
  tome) the card flags.
- **The Control Blocks** → hunt down **Vilenta** (skill point).
  With all three trials done, the party may detour to the
  **Merciless Labyrinth** here — ascendancy points 5 and 6.
- **Torched Courts → The Desecrated Chambers** → kill **Avarius,
  Reassembled** and take the Staff of Purity. Bannon carries it to
  its end in town; Innocence wakes and shatters Kitava's horns.
- **The Canals → The Feeding Trough** → talk to Sin at the far end
  to start the finale.

**Free skill points:** Vilenta's Vengeance (Lani), and after the
fight, An End to Hunger (**+2**, Lani).

**Boss — Kitava, round two:** the Act 5 fight with bigger numbers.
**Portal up in town before the fight** (the card reminds you) — if
you die, take the portal straight back in and continue; the fight
doesn't reset. Sidestep the scythes, kill adds for flask charges,
stay out of the beam. Killing him ends the campaign: **your
resistances drop another 30%** (again: by design — 60% total now),
the party regears for real, and the endgame begins from Karui
Shores with your Ascendancy done and all 24 quest points banked.
Type `/passives` in chat to check — the card does too. *Finish
around level 68.*

## When it feels bad

- **"Everything kills me"** → press C: are resistances near 75? Is
  your life flask from four acts ago? Those two fix 90% of it.
- **"My damage is pathetic"** → gem levels (are your gems still
  socketed and leveling?), then weapon (attackers) — and check the
  card's gem row: a "@" reminder you skipped is usually the culprit.
- **"I'm lost"** → F7 panel, click the shape matching your minimap,
  follow the green line. Or warp to a party member.
- **"The card is on the wrong step"** → F2/F3 until it matches.
- **"I'm slowing everyone down"** → you aren't; the comp was picked
  so the group clears at the speed of its carry, not its newest
  player. Say it out loud anyway and someone will party-portal you
  forward.

Anything else: screenshot → group chat. Enjoy the league.
